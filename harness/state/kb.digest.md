**Organizing idea of the field (the through-line of the shelf):** solving an iterative functional
equation = finding a coordinate in which iteration becomes trivial. The master equation is the
conjugacy equation \(\psi\circ f = g\circ\psi\); its three classical special cases — Schröder
(\(g=s\cdot\), hyperbolic), Abel (\(g=\cdot+1\), parabolic), Böttcher (\(g=(\cdot)^n\),
superattracting) — partition the fixed-point world by multiplier, with Siegel/Cremer (irrationally
indifferent) as the arithmetically delicate fourth case. Iterative roots, fractional/continuous
iteration (embedding in a flow), and conjugacy classification are the central problems; the same
coordinates reappear across complex dynamics, tetration, combinatorics, probability, operators, and RG.
