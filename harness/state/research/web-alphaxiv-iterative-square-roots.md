## Abstract (paraphrase)
An iterative square root of f is g with g(g(·)) = f(·). The paper gives new characterizations for
detecting the **non-existence** of iterative square roots for self-maps on arbitrary sets. Consequence:
continuous self-maps with **no** square roots are **dense** in the space C(X) of all continuous self-maps,
for spaces X homeomorphic to the unit cube in R^m and to all of R^m (every positive integer m).
Conversely, every continuous self-map of a space homeomorphic to the unit cube in R^m **with a fixed point
on the boundary** can be **approximated** by iterative squares of continuous self-maps.

## Framing of the iterative-root problem (useful for the "studies" page)
- General problem: given continuous g:X→X and n∈ℤ⁺, an **iterative root of order n** is continuous f:X→X
  with fⁿ(x)=g(x) ∀x (f⁰=id, fᵏ=f∘fᵏ⁻¹). For n=2 it is an **iterative square root**. (Equation (1).)
- The iterative root problem is **closely related to**: the **embedding flow problem**, the **invariant
  curve problem**, and **linearization**. Of interest in both functional-equation theory and dynamical
  systems; applications cited in statistics, signal processing, computer graphics, information techniques.
- Origins traced to **Babbage [1815], Abel, Koenigs**. Advances surveyed for: continuous maps on intervals,
  esp. **piecewise monotone (PM)** maps (Zhang Weinian and collaborators: Li–Yang–Zhang, Li–Zhang, Lin,
  Lin–Zeng–Zhang, Liu–Jarczyk–Zhang, Liu–Li–Zhang, Liu–Zhang, W. Zhang 1997); continuous complex maps;
  series and transseries (Edgar); continuous maps on planes and Rᵐ (Leśniak, Yu); **set-valued maps**
  (Jarczyk–Zhang, Li, Liu–Li–Zhang). Properties studied: approximation, stability, differentiability,
  category & measure.
