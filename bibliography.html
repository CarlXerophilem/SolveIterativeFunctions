Bibliography & Credits

Primary References

The mathematical core of this site is based on the following papers:

  1. Vladimir V. Kruchinin, Dmitry V. Kruchinin (2013). Composita and its Properties. arXiv:1103.2582v2.
    arXiv:1103.2582 — Introduces the composita \(F^{\Delta}(n,k)\), proves the recurrence formula, and establishes the composition theorem. The foundational paper for the methods used on this site.
  2. Dmitry V. Kruchinin, Vladimir V. Kruchinin (2013). Method for solving an iterative functional equation \(A^{2^n}(x) = F(x)\). arXiv:1302.1986v2.
    arXiv:1302.1986 — Extends the composita method to iterative functional equations, providing explicit formulas for functional square roots and higher iterates via generating functions.

External Resources

  1. Lars Regløs. \(f(f(x))\) — Interactive exploration of functional square roots.
    reglos.de/lars/ffx.html — A visual, interactive investigation of the functional equation \(f(f(x)) = F(x)\) with draggable points and real-time graphing.

Further Reading

  1. R. P. Stanley (1999). Enumerative Combinatorics, Vol. 2. Cambridge Studies in Advanced Mathematics, Vol. 62. Cambridge University Press. — Contains the Lagrange inversion formula and applications to generating functions.
  2. L. Comtet (1974). Advanced Combinatorics. D. Reidel Publishing Company. — Comprehensive treatment of Stirling numbers, Bell polynomials, and formal power series.
  3. R. L. Graham, D. E. Knuth, O. Patashnik (1989). Concrete Mathematics. Addison-Wesley. — Accessible treatment of generating functions, recurrences, and combinatorial sums.
  4. P. Flajolet, R. Sedgewick (2008). Analytic Combinatorics. Cambridge University Press. — Deep theory of generating functions and singularity analysis.
  5. H. S. Wilf (1994). Generatingfunctionology. Academic Press. — Classic introduction to generating functions and their applications.
