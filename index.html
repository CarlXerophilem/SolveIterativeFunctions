What is a Functional Square Root?

Given a function \(F(x)\), a functional square root (or half-iterate) is a function \(f(x)\) satisfying \[ f(f(x)) = F(x). \] Equivalently, \(f^{\circ 2} = F\), where \(f^{\circ n}\) denotes the \(n\)-th iterate of \(f\).

Some equations, like \(f(f(x)) = x^2 - 2\), have elegant closed-form solutions connected to Chebyshev polynomials. Others, like \(f(f(x)) = x^2 + 1\), have no known closed form — their dynamics lack real fixed points and diverge. The Composita method, developed by V.V. and D.V. Kruchinin, provides a systematic framework for analyzing and numerically solving such equations via generating functions.

But the functional square root is only the doorway. For a map of the whole field — its history, its canonical equations from Babbage to Böttcher, and its links to complex dynamics, tetration, and the renormalization group — start at The Shelf.
